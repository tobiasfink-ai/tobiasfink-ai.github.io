Tobias Fink

Data Science | Natural Language Processing | Information Retrieval

I am a PhD student at the TU Wien supervised by Prof. Allan Hanbury and funded by the BRISE Vienna project for which I am developing a semantic search system.

Text processing first piqued my interest during a computer science lecture about social media, where I experimented with the Instagram API. It was unexpected to see, how much information about current events and user behavior could be gained just from looking at hashtag co-occurrence.

With a project about Tweet based Twitter user identification, I continued in this direction and first used machine learning on textual data. This was also when I learned about word embeddings (word2vec), which can express various syntactic and semantic relations with simple vector addition and subtraction. 

With my master thesis I deepened my exploration of natural language processing and investigated information extraction from patent data (CLEF-IP). With further projects in the legal and e-commerce domains, I was able to continue my research on how to effectively and automatically extract information from text data.

With my PhD project I moved in the direction of information retrieval. In this project I am researching ways of recommending court cases which are similar to court cases relevant for building permit issuance.
